Local Considerations

What to Know About This Market

Painting in Belgium has specific commercial and regulatory nuances:

  • BTW rates: The 6% reduced BTW rate applies to renovation work on private residences over 10 years old. Always confirm eligibility with your accountant. For commercial or new-build work, 21% applies.
  • Language: Always ask which language the client prefers for formal documents. Many Brussels clients are happily bilingual, but sending a Dutch quote to a Walloon client (or vice versa) can read as careless.
  • VOC regulations: EU VOC regulations on decorative paints apply in Belgium. Low-VOC and zero-VOC paints are increasingly expected on premium residential projects. Reference this in proposals to signal awareness.
  • Heritage buildings: Flanders, Brussels, and Wallonia all have heritage protection schemes. If working on a listed building, some exterior paint colours or products may require approval. AI can help draft communications to the client about this process.

FAQ

Common Questions

Can I use these prompts to write in both Dutch and French?

Yes. ChatGPT handles both languages reliably. Specify which language you need in the prompt. For bilingual clients, you can even ask for a brief summary in both languages in the same email, though keeping it to one language is usually cleaner.

Does the 6% BTW always apply to renovation work in Belgium?

The 6% reduced rate applies to renovation work on private residences that are at least 10 years old. There are conditions and documentation requirements. Always confirm the correct rate with your accountant before applying it to quotes.

Can AI help me with HVAC or asbestos compliance documentation?

AI can draft communications about asbestos awareness (e.g. asking the client to confirm an asbestos survey has been done on older properties), but actual asbestos handling and compliance require certified professionals. Use it for the communication layer only.

I work in Wallonia. Are these prompts relevant for French-only clients?

Absolutely. The prompts work in French by specifying it in the instruction. The regulatory and cultural details apply across Belgium's regions, with minor differences in heritage protection schemes between Flanders and Wallonia.
