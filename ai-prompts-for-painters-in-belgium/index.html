How Painters in Belgium Are Using AI to Save Time and Grow

Painters in Belgium face a double communication challenge that most other European markets don't: every client email, quote, and follow-up may need to exist in Dutch, French, or both. A client in Liege expects French. A client in Bruges expects Dutch. Get it wrong and the job can go to someone who got it right. AI tools like ChatGPT let you draft professional messages in either language in seconds, without paying for a translator or spending 30 minutes choosing the right words.

The second major friction point is BTW. The standard rate is 21%, but for residential renovation work on properties older than 10 years, Belgian painters can apply the reduced 6% BTW rate. Clients always ask about this. Explaining it clearly in a quote builds trust and prevents delays. The prompts in this guide are built around these real Belgian challenges so you get usable output from the first paste.

Local Considerations

Local Considerations in Belgium

  • Language: Always confirm whether your client prefers Dutch or French before sending documents. In Brussels, many clients are comfortable with both but default to French for formal documents. Use the language placeholder in every prompt.
  • BTW/TVA: Standard rate is 21%. The reduced 6% rate applies to renovation and maintenance of residential properties older than 10 years. Include a clear note in every quote where this applies. Verify eligibility with your accountant for each project.
  • Confederatie Bouw: Membership signals professionalism. Reference it in your quote emails and completion reports where relevant. It matters to clients comparing multiple painters.
  • Customer behaviour: Belgian clients tend to be thorough decision-makers. A clear, well-structured quote with itemised costs and explicit BTW treatment closes faster than a verbal estimate followed by a vague email.
