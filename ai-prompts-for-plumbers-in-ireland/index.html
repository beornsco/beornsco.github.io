Local considerations in Ireland

RGII registration is your lead-closing credential
Irish homeowners must use an RGII-registered contractor for all gas work. Include your RGII number in the first line of every gas-related quote message. Clients who are comparing multiple quotes will choose the contractor who makes compliance easiest.
Ask for WhatsApp reviews, not email reviews
Irish customers respond to WhatsApp review requests at a much higher rate than email. Use the review request prompt as a WhatsApp message. Send it within one hour of completing the job while the customer is still impressed.
RCT applies on commercial sites - add it to your payment terms
Revenue Commissioners require RCT deductions on commercial plumbing subcontracts. Add a standing RCT clause to your commercial payment terms prompt. Facility managers and main contractors expect to see it.
Dublin emergency call-out rates can be higher - state your rate upfront
Emergency plumbing demand in Dublin is high and clients expect premium pricing for out-of-hours work. Use the emergency callout quote prompt to state your out-of-hours rate clearly before arrival. Clients who agree in advance do not dispute the invoice.
FAQ

Common Questions

Should I include my RGII registration number in gas-related quotes?

Yes, in the opening line. Irish homeowners are required by law to use an RGII-registered contractor for gas work. Leads who see your RGII number in the first sentence of your quote almost never ask to see it separately. Those who have to ask for it sometimes go elsewhere.

How do I ask for Google reviews in a way that gets responses in Ireland?

Format the review request prompt as a WhatsApp message and send it within one hour of completing the job. Irish customers who are asked via WhatsApp while still at home respond at a much higher rate than those who receive an email review request the following day.

Can these prompts help me manage RCT on commercial plumbing jobs?

Yes. Add a standing RCT clause to your commercial payment terms prompt: 'Payments to subcontractors are subject to RCT deductions as required by Revenue Commissioners.' This takes 30 seconds to add to your template and prevents the most common payment dispute on Irish commercial plumbing subcontracts.

How do I use these prompts to generate emergency callout quotes with clear out-of-hours rates?

Use the emergency callout quote prompt with the instruction: 'State the out-of-hours rate clearly in the opening line before any job description.' Irish clients who agree to an out-of-hours rate before the plumber arrives almost never dispute the invoice. Clients who find out the rate at the end sometimes do.
