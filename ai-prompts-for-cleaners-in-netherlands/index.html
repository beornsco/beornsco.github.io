Local Considerations

What to Know About This Market

Operating as a cleaning business in the Netherlands has some specific realities these prompts account for:

  • BTW: Standard BTW rate is 21% for cleaning services. Always reference this in quotes. KVK (Kamer van Koophandel) registration is mandatory for any business operating in the Netherlands.
  • Language: Most clients in major cities are comfortable in English, but Dutch communications signal professionalism and local roots. Having both versions ready takes seconds with AI.
  • Tenant law: Dutch rental law (huurrecht) is tenant-friendly. End-of-tenancy charges need solid documentation. Use the dispute prompt alongside a thorough check-in inventory to protect yourself.
  • Directness: Dutch business culture values directness. Don't over-apologise or over-explain in client communications. The prompts are written with this in mind.

FAQ

Common Questions

Should I write in Dutch or English for Dutch clients?

It depends on the client. For local residential clients, Dutch signals professionalism and familiarity. For international clients or expats (common in Amsterdam and The Hague), English is expected. ChatGPT handles both. Specify in the prompt which you need.

Can AI help me price cleaning jobs?

AI can help you write about pricing clearly, but it can't set your rates. Use it to communicate your existing pricing professionally. For market rate benchmarking, check local Facebook cleaning groups or platforms like Werkspot.nl.

Do I need to disclose GDPR compliance to clients?

Yes. Under Dutch/EU law, if you store client data (names, addresses, contact details) you need a basic privacy notice. AI can help draft a simple privacy statement. For proper legal advice, consult an AVG (GDPR) specialist.

What platform should I use these prompts with?

ChatGPT free works for all of these. You can also use Claude or Gemini. Copy the prompt, fill in the brackets, and paste into the chat. No special setup needed.
