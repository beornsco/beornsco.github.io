Day 5 of 5: Hiring Messages and Building Your Prompt System

Day 5 covers hiring and team communications — and shows you how to turn these 5 days into a permanent system.

Today's prompts

Copy, fill in the [brackets], paste into ChatGPT. Under 60 seconds each.

Job Advertisement Hiring

Use this when: You need to hire a tradesperson, labourer, or office/admin person and want a professional job ad.

Write a job advertisement for a [role, e.g. qualified plumber, part-time admin]. Business: [name]. Location: [town/city]. Hours: [full/part time, hours per week]. Pay: [rate or range]. Key requirements: [2-3 things]. What you offer: [2-3 things — include anything that makes the role attractive]. Keep it under 150 words. Direct and clear.
Example output

We are hiring a qualified plumber at Walsh Plumbing, Cork. Full time, Monday to Friday. GBP 22-26 per hour depending on experience. Gas Safe registration essential, experience in domestic installations preferred. We are a 6-person team, good work culture, no weekend callouts. Van and fuel included. Send your CV to jobs@walshplumbing.ie or call 087 345 6789.

Why it works: "No weekend callouts" and "van and fuel included" are specific details that attract better candidates and filter out those who would not stay. Vague job ads get vague applicants. Specific ads get people who are genuinely suited to the role.

Message to Team After a Difficult Job or Day Team

Use this when: Your team has had a hard day, a difficult customer, or a job that went wrong — and you want to acknowledge it without a big speech.

Write a short message to my team after a difficult day or job. Situation: [describe briefly, e.g. customer was difficult, job ran over, something went wrong]. Tone: genuine, brief, not overly corporate. Under 50 words. Acknowledge the difficulty, thank them, move forward.
Example output

Tough one today lads. That client was difficult from the start and you handled it professionally. Job done to standard, which is all we can do. Get some rest. Same again tomorrow. Thanks, Dave.

Why it works: Short messages like this build loyalty over time. You do not need a long speech. Acknowledging what happened — without dramatising it — is enough. This kind of message is remembered.

How to turn 5 days of prompts into a permanent system

You now have prompts covering: quotes, invoices, reviews, social posts, complaints, and hiring. That covers roughly 80% of the written communication a trade business needs. The next step is simple: save these prompts in one place. A notes app, a Google Doc, a text file — anything. Label each one. When you need to write something, open the file, find the closest prompt, fill in the brackets, paste into ChatGPT, edit slightly, send. The time you save is not just the writing time. It is the mental overhead of figuring out what to say. Remove that overhead permanently and the prompts pay for themselves in the first week.
