Day 1 of 5: Write Better Quote Follow-Ups and Review Responses

Two of the most common writing tasks for tradespeople — done in under 2 minutes each.

Today's prompts

Copy, fill in the [brackets], paste into ChatGPT. Under 60 seconds each.

Quote Follow-Up (5-7 Days) Sales

Use this when: A customer received your quote 5-7 days ago and has not responded.

Write a friendly follow-up message for a customer who received a quote [X] days ago. Job: [describe, e.g. bathroom renovation, boiler installation]. Business: [your name]. Quote amount: [amount]. Keep it under 80 words. Warm but professional. End with an open question.
Example output

Hi Paul, just following up on the quote I sent last week for the bathroom renovation (EUR 2,800). Happy to answer any questions or talk through the work involved. Quote is valid for another 10 days. Worth a quick call to chat it through? Cheers, Tom at Walsh Plumbing.

Why it works: Asking an open question at the end gives Paul a reason to respond without committing. It is less pressure than "let me know if you want to proceed" — which often gets ignored.

Google Review Response (Any Rating) Reputation

Use this when: You have received a Google review — positive, negative, or somewhere in between — and need to respond professionally.

Write a professional response to this Google review for my trade business. Review: [paste the review text]. Business name: [name]. Keep it under 80 words. Acknowledge what they said specifically, thank them or address the concern calmly. Do not be defensive.
Example output

Thank you for the honest feedback, Mrs Davies. You are right that communication could have been better during the second week and I apologise for that. The work itself was completed to standard and I am glad you acknowledged that. I have since put a weekly update system in place for all jobs. I hope we can work together again. Mark, Williams Plumbing.

Why it works: Responding to every review — including negative ones — signals to future customers that you take your work seriously. The response is read by potential clients, not just the reviewer.

The system behind these two prompts

Both prompts share a structure: context first (what happened), then tone calibration (how to come across), then constraint (length limit). The length limit is critical. Without it, ChatGPT writes long, formal emails that tradespeople would never actually send. The 80-word limit forces it to match how you actually communicate. Once you have a prompt that works, save it. You will use the same one every week.
