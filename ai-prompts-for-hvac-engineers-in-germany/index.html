Local considerations in Germany

BEG subsidy information in quotes converts heat pump leads
German homeowners can access significant BEG (Bundesfoerderung Effiziente Gebaeude) subsidies for heat pump installations. Add a sentence to every heat pump quote prompt: 'This installation may qualify for BEG subsidies of up to 70%. I can advise on the application process.' Clients who hear about subsidies from you sign faster.
F-Gas certification must appear on refrigerant-related quotes
German law requires F-Gas certification for anyone handling refrigerants. Add your F-Gas certification number to every HVAC quote for refrigerant work. German commercial clients will check this before awarding the contract.
Formal Sie form and Auftragsbestaetigung are mandatory in Germany
German HVAC clients expect formal written order confirmation after every verbal agreement. Use the job confirmation prompt immediately after the call, in formal German with Sie form. Technical clients in facilities management are especially particular about this.
SHK trade body membership signals market credibility
The SHK (Sanitaer Heizung Klima) trade body is the recognised industry association for HVAC in Germany. If you are affiliated, include it in your introduction and commercial quote prompts. Facility managers and property managers use SHK membership as a shortlisting criterion.

Add to every heat pump quote prompt: 'Include a sentence stating that this installation may qualify for BEG (Bundesfoerderung Effiziente Gebaeude) subsidies of up to 70%, and that we can advise on the application.' Should I include F-Gas certification in refrigerant-related quotes for Germany?

Yes. Add your F-Gas certification number to every quote involving refrigerants. German law requires certified engineers for refrigerant handling, and commercial facility managers verify this before awarding contracts. Quotes that include the certification number move straight to approval.

How do I write formal German HVAC communications with the correct register?

Add to every prompt template for German clients: 'Write in formal German using Sie throughout.' German HVAC clients, especially in commercial and facilities management contexts, expect formal written communication. Informal language in quotes or confirmations is a significant trust negative in Germany.

Yes. Add to your introduction and commercial quote prompts: 'Include our SHK (Sanitaer Heizung Klima) affiliation in the opening paragraph.' German facility managers and commercial property managers use SHK membership as a primary shortlisting criterion. Include it in every commercial communication.
