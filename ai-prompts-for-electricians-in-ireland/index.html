Local considerations in Ireland

Your Safe Electric Ireland registration is your lead magnet - say it first
Irish homeowners are aware that electrical work must be done by a Safe Electric registered contractor. Put your RECI or ECSSA registration number in the first line of every quote message. Leads who see this early convert at a much higher rate.
Ask for Google reviews by WhatsApp, not email
Irish customers are significantly more likely to leave a Google review when asked by WhatsApp than by email. Use the review request prompt as a WhatsApp message and send it within an hour of finishing the job while the experience is fresh.
RCT applies if you subcontract - mention it in your payment terms
If you use subcontractors for larger jobs, Revenue Commissioners require you to withhold RCT (Relevant Contracts Tax). Add a sentence to your payment terms prompt about RCT deductions to avoid confused subcontractors and payment disputes.
Dublin and commuter belt demand is high - use location in your social posts
Electrical work demand in Dublin, Kildare, and Wicklow is strong. When writing social posts, use the location prompt variable to name the county or area. Local specificity generates significantly more engagement and enquiries than generic posts.

Should I mention my Safe Electric registration number in quotes generated by these prompts?

Yes, and do it in the first line. Irish homeowners have been advised by consumer campaigns to verify Safe Electric registration before booking any electrical work. Prompts that include your RECI or ECSSA number in the opening sentence convert leads at a significantly higher rate.

Can these prompts help me write notifiable work documentation for Irish Building Regulations?

Yes. Use the project documentation prompt with the instruction: 'This is notifiable domestic electrical work in Ireland. Include a reference to Safe Electric Ireland notification requirements.' Clients who understand their legal obligations and see that you manage the process are far more likely to book.

How do I use these prompts to ask for Google reviews by WhatsApp?

Use the review request prompt and ask ChatGPT to format it as a WhatsApp message, not an email. Send it within one hour of finishing the job. Irish clients who are asked via WhatsApp while still at home leave reviews at a much higher rate than those emailed the next day.

Do the prompts work for commercial electrical quotes involving RCT in Ireland?

Yes. Add to your commercial payment terms prompt: 'Include a clause stating that subcontractor payments are subject to RCT deductions as required by Revenue Commissioners.' Main contractors and facilities managers on commercial sites expect to see this in writing before awarding work.
