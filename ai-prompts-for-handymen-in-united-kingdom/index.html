Local considerations in United Kingdom

Know the Part P limits and state them clearly in enquiry responses
UK handymen are legally limited to minor electrical work such as a fused spur. Being explicit about this in your enquiry responses - and recommending a certified electrician for notifiable work - builds trust and prevents liability. Use the scope clarification prompt to handle this professionally.
Respond to Checkatrade leads within 2 hours
UK handymen on Checkatrade who respond within 2 hours convert leads at a significantly higher rate. Use the quick response prompt from your phone to send a professional reply within minutes of receiving an enquiry.
CIS applies if you subcontract to tradespeople on larger jobs
If you bring in other tradespeople under contract for larger handyman projects, HMRC requires CIS deductions to be managed. Add a CIS clause to your subcontractor payment terms prompt so it appears automatically.
DBS check mention increases domestic client trust
UK homeowners are increasingly asking domestic tradespeople about DBS (criminal record) check status. If you have one, add it to your introduction and quote prompts. One sentence: 'DBS checked. References available on request.' It removes the biggest hesitation for clients working from home.
FAQ

Common Questions

How do I communicate clearly about Part P electrical limits in enquiry responses?

Use the scope clarification prompt with the instruction: 'Explain that UK handymen are legally limited to minor electrical work such as a fused spur, and recommend a qualified electrician for notifiable work.' Being upfront about legal limits builds far more trust than vague answers. Most clients appreciate the honesty.

Should I mention my DBS check in quotes and introduction messages?

Yes. Add to your introduction and quote prompts: 'Include a line stating the operative is DBS checked and references are available on request.' UK homeowners increasingly ask about DBS status before booking domestic tradespeople. Having it in the quote removes the question before it is raised.

How do I respond to Checkatrade enquiries fast enough to win jobs?

Use the quick response prompt from your phone immediately after a Checkatrade notification. A professional reply within 2 hours converts at a much higher rate than a reply sent the next morning. Keep the prompt bookmarked so you can generate a reply in under 3 minutes from anywhere.

Can these prompts help with CIS compliance for larger handyman jobs with subbies?

Yes. Add to your commercial payment terms prompt: 'Include a clause stating that subcontractor payments are subject to CIS deductions under HMRC rules.' Subbies who see this in writing before work starts accept the deduction. Subbies who find out at payment time often dispute it.
