Local considerations in Ireland

C2 certificate from Revenue Commissioners matters for commercial clients
Commercial and property management clients in Ireland ask for a C2 certificate from Revenue Commissioners before awarding larger jobs. If you have one, mention it in your commercial quote intro. If you do not, register with Revenue - it opens access to a much larger client base.
WhatsApp review requests work much better than email in Ireland
Irish clients leave Google reviews at a much higher rate when asked by WhatsApp within an hour of the job finishing. Use the review request prompt as a WhatsApp message, not an email. The difference in response rate is significant.
Know your legal limits on electrical work - and say it clearly
In Ireland, domestic electrical work must be notified by a Safe Electric registered contractor. Being upfront about this in your enquiry responses, and referring clients to a registered electrician for notifiable work, builds credibility rather than costing you the job.
CIRI registration growing - consider registering
The Construction Industry Register Ireland (CIRI) is gaining recognition among homeowners and commercial clients. If you have not registered, it is worth considering. In the meantime, use the trust signals prompt to emphasise your insurance, experience, and completed project portfolio instead.
FAQ

Common Questions

Should I mention my C2 certificate in commercial quotes in Ireland?

Yes. Add to your commercial quote opening: 'Include our C2 certificate details from Revenue Commissioners.' Commercial property managers and larger clients in Ireland require C2 certification for contractor payments. Quotes that include it move straight to the shortlist. Quotes that omit it often trigger an admin query that delays the job.

How do I request Google reviews in a way that gets responses from Irish clients?

Format the review request prompt as a WhatsApp message and send it within one hour of completing the job. Irish clients who are asked by WhatsApp while still at home with the finished work visible respond at a much higher rate than those who receive an email review request the following day.

Can these prompts help me explain the limits of handyman electrical work in Ireland?

Yes. Use the scope clarification prompt with the instruction: 'Explain professionally that notifiable domestic electrical work in Ireland requires a Safe Electric registered contractor, and offer to recommend one.' This approach builds client trust and often generates referrals rather than losing the lead entirely.

How do I use social posts to generate leads in specific Irish counties?

Use the social post prompt with a specific county or suburb: 'completed in [area], [county].' Posts referencing 'Swords, Dublin' or 'Ballincollig, Cork' generate far more local leads than generic Ireland posts. Handymen who name their exact service area in posts get direct calls from neighbours.
