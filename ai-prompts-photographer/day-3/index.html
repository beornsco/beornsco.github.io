Day 3 of 5: Deliver Galleries with a Message Clients Remember

Delivery messages shape the final impression and open natural space for print sales or referrals.

View all 5 days

Today's prompts

Copy, fill in the [brackets], paste into ChatGPT.

Gallery Delivery EmailPrompt

Use this when: You are sending the final gallery link for a wedding or portrait session.

Write a gallery delivery email. Photographer: Sarah Mitchell Photography. Clients: Emma and James. Session type: wedding. Include gallery link placeholder [link], download deadline 30 days, and one line inviting favorites for album design. Warm and celebratory. Under 170 words.
Example output

Hi Emma and James, your gallery is ready and I am so happy to share it with you. You can view and download everything here: [link]. The gallery stays active for 30 days, so please download your full set this week if possible. If you mark your favorite images, I can draft a first album layout for you to review. Thank you again for trusting me with such an important day. Sarah

Why it works: The message gives clear action steps and a gentle next offer without sounding salesy.

Gallery Follow-Up After 5 DaysPrompt

Use this when: You want a polite nudge after gallery delivery.

Write a five-day follow-up message after gallery delivery. Business: Sarah Mitchell Photography. Clients: Emma and James. Ask if they had any issues downloading and invite questions about prints. Under 95 words.
Example output

Hi Emma and James, just checking in to make sure the gallery link is working smoothly for you and that downloads are easy on your side. If you want help choosing print sizes or wall layouts, I am happy to guide you. Sarah

Why it works: A service-focused check-in increases trust and often leads to additional orders.

Social Sharing Permission RequestPrompt

Use this when: You want consent before posting client images online.

Write a short email asking permission to share 3 to 5 images from a family session on Instagram and website portfolio. Photographer: Sarah Mitchell Photography. Client: Laura Benson. Respectful and easy to answer yes or no. Under 90 words.
Example output

Hi Laura, I loved editing your family session and would be grateful to share 3 to 5 images on my Instagram and website portfolio. I only post photos you are comfortable with. Totally fine if you prefer to keep everything private. Just reply yes or no and I will follow your preference. Thank you, Sarah

Why it works: Simple permission language protects trust and gets faster replies.

Micro-system insight

Gallery delivery: every strong message has the same pattern. Add context, set tone, set length, and ask for one next action. Save your best prompt once, then only swap names, dates, and details.

Localisation bridge

Add 'Write the response in Dutch/French/German' to any prompt. The structure stays the same, only the language changes.
