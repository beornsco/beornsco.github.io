Package Explanation EmailPrompt

Use this when: A lead asks for pricing and what is included.

Write a pricing email for wedding photography packages. Business: Sarah Mitchell Photography. Client: Emma and James. Include three options: Essential EUR 900, Signature EUR 1400, Full Day EUR 1900. Briefly explain what each includes and who it fits. Invite a call. Under 190 words.
Example output

Hi Emma and James, thanks for asking about pricing. I offer three options. Essential: EUR 900 for 4 hours, ideal for ceremony and portraits. Signature: EUR 1400 for 8 hours, covering key moments from prep to first dance. Full Day: EUR 1900 for up to 12 hours with full story coverage and priority editing. All packages include edited high-resolution images and private gallery delivery. If you share your rough timeline, I can recommend the best fit and we can do a quick call this week. Sarah

Why it works: Clients choose faster when options are simple, comparable, and tied to outcomes.

Handle Budget Objection GracefullyPrompt

Use this when: A lead says your pricing is above budget.

Write a respectful response when a wedding lead says the package is over budget. Photographer: Sarah Mitchell Photography. Client names: Emma and James. Keep relationship positive, offer one lighter alternative, and leave the door open. Under 120 words.
Example output

Hi Emma and James, thank you for being open about budget. I completely understand. If it helps, I can offer a shorter 3 hour coverage option focused on ceremony and portraits so you still have strong core memories documented. If timing or budget changes later, I would be happy to revisit the fuller package. Either way, I appreciate you considering me. Sarah

Why it works: You protect brand positioning while still offering a practical next step.
