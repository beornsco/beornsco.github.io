Overview

That creates a commercial opportunity, but it also creates a communication challenge: the more complex the job, the more critical it is to document the specification clearly, agree waterproofing standards before work starts, and handle the inevitable builder-tiler interface disputes professionally.

Tilers operating in Ireland's current market are not just craftspeople. They're specification advisors, dispute navigators, and technical communicators. AI handles the written communication layer so you can focus on the technical execution.

Why It Matters

Why Specification Clarity Matters for Irish Tilers

A tiler in Dublin described a dispute that cost him two weeks of unpaid work. The builder on a bathroom project had specified a standard adhesive, but the substrate turned out to be a heated screed requiring a flexible polymer-modified adhesive. The tiler flagged this on-site, substituted the correct product, and completed the job. The builder refused to pay the material upcharge, claiming it wasn't in the original quote.

The tiler had no written record of flagging the substrate issue. No email, no WhatsApp confirmation. Just a verbal conversation. The dispute dragged on for six weeks. The lesson: technical substitution notifications need to be sent in writing, immediately, every time. AI makes that 3-minute task so easy there's no excuse not to do it.

Material Substitution Notification

Reference the original contract/quote reference if available. Tone: professional and technically precise.

Local Considerations

What to Know About This Market

Ireland's construction environment in 2026 has specific characteristics relevant to tiling work:

  • Construction boom: Dublin and major urban centres are experiencing high construction activity. This means quality tilers have negotiating power, but also means builder interface management is critical on fast-moving sites.
  • VAT: VAT on construction services in Ireland is 13.5% (reduced rate). For private residential clients, include this clearly. Reverse charge VAT applies on subcontracting to VAT-registered businesses (RCT system via Revenue).
  • Standards: Irish tiling work references I.S. EN 12004 (adhesive classification) and I.S. EN 13888 (grout classification). Referencing these in technical communications signals professionalism to architects and main contractors.
  • Wet rooms: Irish building control authorities have been tightening requirements on wet room waterproofing. Document your waterproofing system in writing on every wet room project, even on small domestic jobs.

FAQ

Common Questions

Should I reference Irish building standards in my quotes?

Yes, where relevant. Referencing I.S. EN standards for adhesive and grout classification, and NHBC or building control requirements for wet rooms, demonstrates technical competence to architects, main contractors, and informed homeowners.

What's the RCT system and do I need to reference it?

Relevant Contract Tax (RCT) is Ireland's tax withholding system for construction subcontractors. If you're working as a subcontractor to a main contractor, you're in the RCT system. AI can help draft communications referencing RCT status, but your tax obligations are managed through Revenue's eRCT system.

Can I use AI to help write a technical specification for an architect?

Yes. AI is good at drafting technical specification documents from bullet-point inputs. Provide the product names, classifications, and standards you're working to, and ask it to produce a specification document. Always review the technical details before submitting to a professional.

Are these prompts suitable for commercial tiling projects?

Absolutely. The specification confirmation, material substitution, and dispute record prompts are particularly valuable on commercial projects where the paper trail matters most. Adjust the tone to formal contract language as appropriate.
